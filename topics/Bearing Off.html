Bearing Off

Once all chips are in a player's home quadrant, they can bear off - or take their chips off the backgammon board - to win the game.

The player can remove the chip values shown on the dice. For example, if a player rolls a four and a three, they can remove one chip in the third and fourth space from their side of the board.



If there are no chips in the space and dice value exceeds the furthest chip from the side of the board, remove the highest chip on the board. For example, if a player rolls a six and five, but they have one chips in space 6, but none in space 5, they can remove a chip in space 4.



If there are no chips in the space and dice value is smaller than the furthest chip from the side of the board, move the chip that many spaces forward. For example, if a player rolls a 2 but does not have a chip in that space, they should move any other chip forwards two spaces.



If one of your pieces is captured, you can not continue to bear off until the chip is returned to your home quadrant.
