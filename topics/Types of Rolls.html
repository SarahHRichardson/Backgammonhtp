Types of Rolls

There are three common types of rolls in Backgammon - the standard roll, doubles, and acey-deucey (optional). Each roll is explained in the figure below.

Table 1. Types of Rolls
Roll Type Description Image
Standard For a standard roll, each number on the dice corresponds to the number of spaces the player can move their chip. For example, if a player rolls a 4 and 5, they can either move one chip 4 spaces and another chip 5 spaces, or move one chip 9 spaces.

Doubles If both dice show the same value, the roll is referred to as a double. If a player rolls a double, they can move that number four times. For example, if a player rolls a double 4, they can move four chips 4 spaces or another combination of four 4s.

Some players will use an optional (to be decided before playing) rule that players take an extra turn (re-rolls their dice) after playing the double roll. If that player is unable to play the double roll (due to being blocked by the other player's chips), they forfeit this extra turn.

Acey-Deucey When playing the Acey Deucey variation of Backgammon, if a player rolls a 1 and 2, that roll is called acey-deucey. If a player rolls a 1 and 2, they must first move two chips 1 and 2 spaces (or one chip 3 spaces), then they can take any doubles roll that they want. After taking the doubles, they re-roll the dice for another turn. If the player cannot move a 1 and 2 due to being blocked by the other player's chips, they cannot take the doubles and their turn ends.

Some players incorporate the acey-deucey roll into their standard backgammon games. If players opt into this option, they must also take an extra turn after rolling doubles.
